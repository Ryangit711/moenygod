# How Money Flows in the System

## The Plumbing Nobody Teaches You

Most people think money flows like water — evenly, naturally, following gravity. It doesn't. Money flows through **pipes that were built by people with intent**. Understanding the plumbing is the difference between being water and being the plumber.

---

## The Central Bank: Where All Flow Begins

The central bank (Fed, ECB, BOJ, etc.) is the pump at the center of the system. Everything it does determines the pressure and direction of money flow:

| Action | What It Does | Where Money Flows |
|--------|--------------|-------------------|
| **Lower interest rates** | Makes borrowing cheaper | Money flows OUT of savings → INTO risk assets (stocks, real estate, crypto) |
| **Raise interest rates** | Makes borrowing expensive | Money flows OUT of risk assets → INTO safe assets (bonds, cash) |
| **Quantitative Easing (QE)** | Central bank buys bonds from banks | Banks get cash → lend more → money flows INTO economy and markets |
| **Quantitative Tightening (QT)** | Central bank sells bonds or lets them mature | Cash leaves system → money flow DRIES UP → risk assets fall |
| **Discount window / repo operations** | Emergency lending to banks | Keeps the pipes from freezing during a crisis |

**The single most important thing to track: the direction of central bank policy.** Is the pump pushing money in or pulling it out? Everything else follows.

## The Three Pools Where Money Accumulates

### Pool 1: The Banking System

Banks are the first recipients of new money. When the Fed does QE, it buys bonds FROM banks. Banks then decide where to push that money:

- **Lending to businesses** → corporate expansion → jobs → wage growth
- **Lending to consumers** → mortgages, credit cards → real estate and consumer spending
- **Buying financial assets** → stocks, bonds, derivatives → asset price inflation
- **Parking at the Fed as excess reserves** → earns interest → does nothing for the real economy

**Key insight:** When banks choose financial assets over lending, money flows UP (to asset holders) rather than OUT (to the real economy). This is why QE inflated stock markets while wages stagnated.

### Pool 2: The Government

Government spending is the second major flow channel:

- **Military/defense** → flows to defense contractors and their shareholders
- **Infrastructure** → flows to construction, materials, engineering firms
- **Social programs** → flows to recipients → immediate consumption → retail, food, healthcare
- **Interest on debt** → flows to bondholders (the wealthy)

**Key insight:** When the government borrows and spends, it creates demand. The question is: demand for WHAT, and WHO captures the revenue? If spending is on military, defense stocks are where the flow goes. If on infrastructure, materials and construction. Follow the spending bill, follow the money.

### Pool 3: Financial Markets

Stocks, bonds, real estate, crypto, commodities, derivatives — these are where excess money pools when it has nowhere productive to go:

| When money flows here | What happens |
|----------------------|--------------|
| **Stock market** | P/E ratios expand, indices rise, "wealth effect" makes people spend more |
| **Bond market** | Yields fall, borrowing becomes cheaper, more debt issued |
| **Real estate** | Property prices rise, rent increases, wealth transfers from renters to owners |
| **Commodities** | Raw material prices rise, inflation hits consumers |
| **Crypto** | Speculative bubble, extreme volatility, early adopters profit |

## The Minsky Cycle: How Money Flow Creates Bubbles and Crashes

Hyman Minsky identified a repeating pattern in how money flows through the system:

### Phase 1: Hedge Finance
- Borrowers can pay both interest AND principal from cash flow
- Stable, productive lending
- Money flows into real economic activity
- **This is oikonomia.**

### Phase 2: Speculative Finance
- Borrowers can pay interest but must ROLL OVER principal
- Lending becomes less productive, more about asset speculation
- Money flows into financial assets rather than production
- **This is the transition to chrematistike.**

### Phase 3: Ponzi Finance
- Borrowers can pay NEITHER interest nor principal from cash flow
- They depend on rising asset prices to refinance
- Money flows into pure speculation — "greater fool" dynamics
- **This is chrematistike gone pathological.**

### The Crash
- Asset prices stop rising → Ponzi borrowers default → banks panic → credit freezes → everyone who relied on rolling over debt is destroyed
- Money flow reverses violently: risk assets → cash → safe havens
- This is 2008. This is every bubble burst in history.

**Minsky'sKey insight:** Stability itself creates instability. The longer the boom, the more speculative the lending becomes, because success breeds complacency. "Everyone is making money, so the risk must be low" — until it isn't.

## How to Track Money Flow in Real Time

| Indicator | What It Shows | Where to Find It |
|-----------|---------------|-------------------|
| **M2 money supply** | Total money in the system | FRED (fred.stlouisfed.org) |
| **Fed balance sheet** | How much QE/QT is happening | FRED |
| **Interest rates (Fed Funds, 10Y, 2Y)** | Cost of money, yield curve shape | TradingView, Bloomberg |
| **Yield curve inversion (2Y > 10Y)** | Recession predictor — when short-term rates exceed long-term | FRED |
| **Credit spreads** (junk bond vs. Treasury) | Risk appetite — narrow = greedy, wide = fearful | FRED, ICE BofA indices |
| **Bank lending data** | Are banks lending or hoarding? | Fed H.8 report (weekly) |
| **Insider trading** | Are executives buying or selling their own stock? | OpenInsider.com |
| **Options flow / unusual activity** | Where big money is placing large bets | UnusualWhales.com |
| **Institutional 13F filings** | What hedge funds actually hold (quarterly) | SEC EDGAR |
| **COT report (Commitments of Traders)** | Futures market positioning — who is long, who is short | CFTC.gov |
| **TIPs / breakeven inflation** | Market's inflation expectations | FRED |

## The Flow Map: Where Money Goes After the Fed Acts

```
Central Bank
    │
    ├── Lower rates / QE
    │       │
    │       ▼
    │   Banks get cash
    │       │
    │       ├── Lend to corps → capital expenditure → jobs → wages → consumption
    │       ├── Lend to consumers → mortgages → real estate prices UP
    │       ├── Buy financial assets → stocks UP, bonds UP
    │       └── Park at Fed → excess reserves → nothing (the "pushing on a string" problem)
    │
    └── Raise rates / QT
            │
            ▼
        Cash leaves system
            │
            ├── Borrowing gets expensive → growth slows → layoffs
            ├── Risk assets sell off → stocks DOWN, crypto DOWN
            ├── Money flows to safety → Treasuries UP, USD UP
            └── Credit tightens → small businesses die → consolidation by big players
```

---

## The One Rule

**Don't fight the Fed.** When the pump is pushing money in, be in risk assets. When the pump is pulling money out, be in cash or safe assets. This single principle, followed consistently, captures more of the money flow than any other strategy.

Everything else — sectors, stocks, crypto, gold — is secondary to this primary flow.
